I'm not sure what I want do do with my life,
but here's what I do know.


I know that a sudden change from "no engineering!" to "engineering!" seems odd and out of place, but I think it is a very logical choice for me.

I have always excelled at math and science. I avoided physics because I was scared it would be the thing I wasn't good at, but I am taking it now and it is fun! Plus, engineering doesn't necessarily mean building bridges. There are so many fields with ways I can incorporate math, chemistry, the environment, computer science, or other interests I may find.

I enjoyed taking Environmental Science last year, and it is a crucial field for maintaining our planet and sustaining human and other life. To be able to play a role in promoting sustainable energy and resource consumption would make a positive difference in the world, something I hope to do in my career.

I know that college is a time for experimentation, and I may not stick with anything that I pick. However, I am trying to narrow down my selection to something that I will excel at: and I believe math-based science will be a good fit for me.

While I was excited by the idea of teaching, I beleive it will be too stressful for me. There is a lot of change that I will have to cope with, something that is very difficult for me to do. Plus, I think that I have an idea of what teaching may look like based on the peers in my own classes. However, that is not necessarily the reality of teaching high school, and I believe that will add extra issues.

That's not to say that I can't or won't ever be a teacher. Maybe twenty years from now, when I have kids, teaching will seem like the best option for me. It will be better for me to get experience in the field I study before I teach whatever I may choose to. Plus, it will be a better use of college time and money because I can get a teaching degree easier than I could an engineering degree.

I will continue to explore my options and narrow down what I would like to do. But I believe environmental engineering will be a good way for me to make a solid salary and live comfortably out of college. Plus, it will give me a good support system for when I am ready to start my own family.
